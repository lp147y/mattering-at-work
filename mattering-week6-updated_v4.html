Self-Compassion · Sustainability

You Cannot Give What You Do Not Have: Tending to Your Own Mattering

Five weeks of this campaign have focused outward — on how we communicate significance to others. This final week turns inward, because Mercurio is clear on something that is easy to overlook in healthcare and academic cultures steeped in self-sacrifice: you also need to feel you matter.

Campaign Principle

We cannot sustainably create mattering for others if our own sense of mattering is depleted. Looking after ourselves is not selfishness; it is part of sustaining compassionate and psychologically safe cultures.

Veterinary professionals and academics often carry a cultural script that frames self-acknowledgement as vanity and self-care as indulgence. We are trained to focus on others — patients, students, the science, the mission. But if mattering requires being noticed and valued, many of us are running on a deficit.

And a person running on a mattering deficit is more depleted, less resilient, and ultimately less able to matter to others.

The Honest Inventory — tick each as you sit with it

Take a few minutes with these questions — privately, honestly:

In your current role, do you feel genuinely noticed — by someone whose opinion you respect — for who you are, not just what you produce?
Do you believe your specific presence in your team is significant — that if you were gone, something irreplaceable would be missing?
Is there someone in your professional life you could ask to reflect back to you what they specifically value in your presence — and would you be willing to ask?

Six Weeks On: What Has Shifted?

Before we close with a commitment, it is worth pausing to integrate what you have covered. Tap each card to revisit the key insight from each week.

🌱

Week 1: What Mattering Is

tap to revisit

Mattering = being noticed + being important. Not a reward for performance. A baseline human need. The first step is learning to see the gaps.

🧠

Week 2: The Mattering Mindset

tap to revisit

Mattering is communicated in everyday micro-moments: names used, questions asked, voices invited. Meeting architecture is culture made visible.

🔥

Week 3: Mattering and Burnout

tap to revisit

Feeling invisible is neurologically costly. Mattering deficits amplify burnout and impair team communication — making this a patient safety issue, not only a wellbeing one.

🌍

Week 4: Mattering Across Difference

tap to revisit

Mattering is not distributed equally. Those with less power, those navigating cultural difference, those in lower-status roles — all are at greater risk. Witnessing their significance in real time is allyship.

🏗️

Week 5: Building the Culture

tap to revisit

Individual generosity is fragile. Institutional infrastructure is durable. Recognition systems, feedback loops, equitable development — these embed mattering in the culture's architecture.

💙

Week 6: You Matter Too

tap to revisit

Tending to your own sense of significance is not selfishness — it is sustainability. You cannot give what you do not have. Your mattering matters.

Commitment · What Next

What Will You Carry Forward?

Campaigns end. Cultures are built over years, through thousands of small, deliberate choices. What follows is not a tick-box exercise — it is an invitation to commit, privately, to one or two practices that you will genuinely try to sustain.

Tick only the ones that feel honest — not aspirational.

My Commitments — tick what you genuinely intend to carry forward
I will greet colleagues by name — specifically, personally — rather than addressing the room collectively.
I will make at least one specific, effort-based acknowledgement each week — naming what I noticed and why it mattered.
When I notice someone's voice being overlooked, I will name it — calmly and specifically — in the moment.
A Final Word

You have spent six weeks attending to something that most workplaces leave entirely to chance. Every person who completes this campaign carries something forward that has the potential to change — small moment by small moment — what it feels like to work here. That is not nothing. That is the work.

Some of Our Staff — The People Behind the Care

Small moments, big impact — staff connecting with patients

The care behind the care — every interaction counts

From the clinic to the farm — our team, wherever the work takes them

These are just some of the people who make a difference every day — residents, interns, nurses, ACAs, clinicians, receptionists, students, facilities management professionals, administrative and support staff, and everyone in between. Every role matters. Every person matters.

People do not only need to belong. They need to matter. They need to know they are noticed, affirmed, and needed. Our challenge is to make that belief possible — not only through kindness, but through the way our teams, systems and institutions work.
